About Electronic Waste

Electronic waste, or e-waste, refers to discarded electronic devices that have reached the end of their useful life. The rapid advancement of technology has increased e-waste generation, creating environmental and health challenges worldwide.

The Problem of E-Waste

E-waste often contains hazardous materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium, which can pollute the environment if not disposed of properly. As electronics become obsolete faster, managing e-waste responsibly has become critical.

Our Mission

Our mission is to spread awareness about the impacts of e-waste and to promote sustainable practices like recycling and reusing electronic devices. Through education, we hope to reduce the environmental footprint of discarded electronics.
